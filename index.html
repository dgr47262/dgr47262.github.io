Hawaii

Overview

Out of all of the 50 states of the U.S, Hawaii can be counted as one of the most beautiful. It contains some of the most diverse ecosystems as well as some of the most amazing views. Ever since it became a state in the year 1959, it has become a major tourist attraction and vacation spot. In this website I will explain the history of the 50th state as well as some facts about this wonderful island.

History

Like mentioned before, the island of Hawaii was made a state in the year 1959. However the history of this island stretches much farther than the 60 years since it became a state. The island was first inhabited by humans around 1000 A.D and they lived in isolation for hundreds of year until discovered by British explorer James Cook in 1778. Hawaii was then made a territory by 1898 and finally made a state by 1959.

Tourism

One of the largest businesses in Hawaii has to be there tourism. Millions of people from around the world visit the islands of Hawaii to see there natural beauty. These attractions include:

  • Volcanoes
  • Beaches
  • Wilderness/Wildlife
  • Historical Sites (Pearl Harbor)

The island of Oahu is the most popular island of Hawaii due to it being the home of the state capital(Honolulu), as well as the site of Pearl harbor which many visit to pay respect to the many victims of that attack. Maui provides some of the most beautiful sites in the whole state with huge waterfalls and deep, green valleys that make it look like an island paradise. Kauai is known as the "garden island" for its dense tropical forests and white sandy beaches. All of the islands, though similar to one another, provide their own incredible experience.
